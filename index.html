Sketch narrative: Food Tracking Clock

This sketch is designed for someone looking to track their daily nutrition and eating habits throughout a full 24-hour cycle. It visualizes the relationship between the time of day and typical meal windows (Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner) to help the user stay mindful of their "eating periods" versus fasting or resting periods.

  • Context: This visualization is intended for a kitchen or dining environment, potentially used as a smart-home interface or a "food-focused" Pomodoro timer to help users maintain a consistent fasting schedule.
  • Design decisions: I chose a 24-hour layout to eliminate AM/PM ambiguity, ensuring the user clearly sees where they are in the full span of a day. I implemented semi-transparent arcs on the plate rim (light green) to provide an immediate at-a-glance status of whether it is currently mealtime
  • Future work: I could make it even more interactive by having the user be able to drag the arc so that they aren't limited to just eating at a certain time period from one time to another.

Sketch narrative: Moldy Cheese

This sketch visualizes time through the lens of entropy and organic decay, in the form of a cheese block. Each new fly on the clock represents an hour, and every hour, the cheese becomes green. After an hour passes, the cheese resets to yellow, a new fly is added, and the cycle repeats.

  • Context: This visualization explores the concept of "entropy" and time as a destructive force. It is designed for an artistic or experimental context, moving away from the precise, mechanical nature of standard clocks to show time as an organic, decaying process of spoiled food.
  • Design decisions: I mapped the hour to the number of flies over the period of a 24 hour clock. This allows the user to see the hour "getting older" as the mold grows darker and more visible. To represent seconds, I made it so that after an hour, the entire cheese block becomes green (to indicate spoilage).
  • Future work: I could make it so that after a full 24-hour day, the cheese clock becomes a solid green color, or to also add some grey/white colors for the fuzz to indicate real spoilage.

Sketch narrative: The Popcorn Clock

This sketch transforms the passage of time into a dynamic popcorn cooking process. It metaphors the "pressure" of time as heat applied to a pot, visualizing the minutes as potential energy (kernels) that eventually transforms into hours (popcorn).

  • Context: This visualization is designed for a casual kitchen environment or a playful smart-home display. It moves away from strict digital precision to offer a "satisfying" and whimsical visual metaphor, framing the accumulation of time as a physical transformation (cooking).
  • Design decisions: I distinguished the hierarchy of time by physical state rather than just size: unpopped kernels represent minutes (potential energy) accumulating at the bottom, while popped corn represents hours (finished product) floating at the top. To visualize seconds without a traditional hand, I implemented a vibration animation. The unpopped kernels shake with increasing intensity as the second count rises, visually simulating the rising heat and pressure before the next minute "pops."
  • Future work: I would like to add a distinct "pop" sound effect that triggers exactly when the minute variable increments. Additionally, I could implement a "burnt" state where the popcorn slowly darkens if the sketch has been running for more than 12 hours.
